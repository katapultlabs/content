# Companion Article Brief: The Harness in Practice (Working Title)

**Relationship to Harness Thesis:** The Thesis is the *why*. This piece is the *how*.

**Core idea:** What does it actually look like when a founder sits down and builds through a harness? Walk through the stack, the workflow, the tools, the handoff points.

**Key beats to cover:**
- The founder-side workflow: high-velocity dreaming captured (Granola, Whisper Flow), transcript fed into Claude Cowork, v1 PRD and architecture generated in minutes
- The harness stack: Claude Code, Cowork, Codex, Cursor, Linear, CI/CD, agents for testing/regression/deploy monitoring
- The PR handoff: founder creates a pull request, harness (agents + humans) hardens it
- What "frictionless setup" actually means: what does day 1 look like for a founder getting wired into the harness?
- Real workflow example: two co-founders on a 30-minute call → Granola transcript → Claude Cowork → PRD → architecture sketch → first PR. No meetings with the team.

**Tone:** Tactical, specific, tool names and workflows. Less philosophy, more "here's what you do on Monday morning."

**Length target:** Shorter than the Thesis. ~800-1,000 words.

**Source material:** Katapult/Avara call (Brad/Alex), Anand sync, internal Katapult workflows.
